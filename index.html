Mission

Finca Lim√≥n embodies the principles of sustainable farming, pursuing a permaculture agroforestry approach. Our primary goal is to reforest, regenerate, and enrich topsoil, thereby contributing positively to nature while also harvesting produce.

Located in the booming town of Santa Teresa de Cobano, renowned for its surf culture and high demand for sustainable, quality food, we're perfectly positioned to serve private chefs, restaurants, and local farmers' markets.

We also organize events and workshops designed to welcome visitors who wish to learn about and engage with sustainable farming practices, creating a bridge between traditional agriculture and regenerative methods.

Who I am

Lorenz Reichlin - Founder and Farmer

I am Lorenz Reichlin, 28 years old, striving to become a farmer of the new generation. Born and raised on a farm in Switzerland, I discovered my passion for plants early on. My parents, a game warden and primary school principal, managed our farm with a big vegetable garden alongside my three siblings.

Growing up surrounded by nature, I developed a deep appreciation for sustainable living and became determined to have my own farm. I have great memories of the incredible home-grown food my mother cooked - a gift everyone should experience.

To fund my dream of traveling and saving for this ultimate goal, I worked on construction sites in Switzerland, acquiring valuable manual skills in construction and landscape design that now serve my farming endeavors.

Environmental Impact

Soil Regeneration

Mimicking natural ecosystems by integrating native and cultivated plants to rejuvenate depleted soil and create sustainable topsoil rich in organic matter.

Biodiversity Enhancement

Creating balanced ecosystems with 350+ native trees planted, supporting local wildlife and establishing food forests as productive biodiversity hubs.

Water Conservation

Implementing water harvesting systems, swales, and natural infiltration methods to maintain healthy soil and reduce demand on local water supplies.

Carbon Sequestration

Dense vegetation and multi-layered food forests maximize photosynthesis, with biomass recycling through composting for efficient carbon storage.

Circular Economy

Zero waste approach using sustainable construction, composting systems, and working with natural cycles to turn waste into valuable resources.

Measurable Progress

Already visible transformation after one year, with 100+ species planted, 60+ surviving, and continuous monitoring through satellite imagery and biodiversity tracking.
